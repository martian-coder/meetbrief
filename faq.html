MeetBrief / FAQ

Common questions,
straight answers

Everything you need to know before installing. If your question isn't here, email martian.coders.x@gmail.com.

Compatibility
Does it work with Zoom, Teams, and Google Meet?
Yes. MeetBrief captures system audio — whatever is playing through your speakers or headphones. This means it works with any meeting app: Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, Webex, Slack huddles, Discord, phone calls through speakers, or any other audio source. No app integration required.
What operating systems are supported?
macOS (Apple Silicon and Intel) is best supported. System audio capture uses a native subprocess. Screen Recording permission is required. Windows works via WASAPI loopback audio capture. Linux works with microphone input by default; system audio capture requires a PulseAudio loopback module.
Does it only listen to the other person, or can it capture me too?
By default MeetBrief captures system audio (speaker output) — which is the remote participant's voice, not yours. This is intentional: you want suggestions for responding to what they said, not transcription of your own words. Microphone input can be enabled in settings.
Can I use it in in-person meetings or phone calls?
Yes. If you enable microphone input, MeetBrief can transcribe voices from the room or a speakerphone. For phone calls played through speakers, system audio capture picks it up automatically. The quality depends on ambient noise — a quiet room with a good microphone works well.
Pricing & Cost
How much does MeetBrief cost?
MeetBrief includes a 25-day free trial with no limitations. After the trial, a one-time license is required. Email martian.coders.x@gmail.com with the subject "MeetBrief License" to get a key. You'll receive a MEET-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX format license key to enter directly in the app.
What are the ongoing API costs?
API costs depend on your provider and usage. With Anthropic Claude and prompt caching enabled, a one-hour meeting typically costs under $0.10 in API calls. Without caching it would be approximately $0.50–$0.80. With Ollama (fully local), there is no per-call cost beyond electricity and compute.
Can I use it without an Anthropic account?
Yes. Set LLM_PROVIDER=ollama in your .env and run any model locally via Ollama. No API key needed and no per-call cost. You'll need a reasonably capable GPU or Apple Silicon CPU for acceptable latency — smaller models like Mistral 7B or Llama 3.1 8B work well on modern hardware.
Privacy
Does MeetBrief record or store my meetings?
Audio is never recorded or written to disk during a session — it's processed in memory by Whisper and discarded after transcription. If you enable Session History in settings, the text transcript and AI suggestions are saved locally to your userData folder. Nothing is ever uploaded to any server.
Does my API key leave my machine?
Your API key is sent only to api.anthropic.com — the official Anthropic API endpoint — in the Authorization header of HTTPS requests. It is never sent to any MeetBrief endpoint (there isn't one). It is stored locally and not included in any other outbound traffic.
Will the other person know I'm using MeetBrief?
MeetBrief makes no attempt to be stealthy — that's an explicit non-goal. The overlay is visible on your screen. Whether the other person can see it depends entirely on your screen sharing settings. If you're not sharing your screen, they won't see the overlay.
Does the app send usage analytics or crash reports?
No. MeetBrief contains no analytics SDK, no crash reporter (like Sentry or Crashlytics), and no usage telemetry. The app makes no background network calls. You can verify this by inspecting the source code and searching for fetch( or axios calls.
Features & Usage
What languages does it support?
Whisper supports 99 languages for transcription. With the translate task enabled (the default), speech in any language is automatically converted to English text before being sent to the LLM. This means you can use MeetBrief for multilingual meetings — a French speaker, a Mandarin speaker, and an Arabic speaker can all be transcribed and responded to from a single session.
How do I control when a suggestion is generated?
By default, suggestions are triggered automatically when Silero VAD detects approximately 1 second of silence after speech — meaning the other person has finished their turn. You can also force-generate at any time using the configurable hotkey. The auto-trigger can be disabled in settings.
How good are the AI suggestions?
Quality depends heavily on the context you load before the meeting. A detailed system prompt — including your role, objectives, the other party's likely concerns, and key talking points — produces much better suggestions than a blank or sparse context. With good context and Claude, suggestions are typically relevant and usable within 1–2 seconds of the speaker finishing.
Can I adjust the overlay position and appearance?
Yes. The overlay position, size, and opacity are adjustable in settings. It defaults to the bottom-right corner of your screen at medium opacity. You can move it to any screen position and control whether it auto-hides when not showing new content.
Does it work during screen sharing?
Yes, it continues capturing and generating suggestions during screen sharing. Whether the overlay is visible in your screen share depends on your meeting app and settings. In Zoom for example, you can share a specific window instead of your full desktop to exclude the overlay from what others see.
Trial & Licensing
How long is the free trial?
The trial is 25 calendar days from first installation. All features are fully available during the trial with no limitations. After 25 days, the app shows a lock screen until a license key is entered. The trial timer is based on the installation date and cannot be reset by reinstalling.
How do I get a license after the trial expires?
Email martian.coders.x@gmail.com with the subject "MeetBrief License". You'll receive a license key in the format MEET-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX. Enter it in the text field on the trial-expired screen and click Activate — the app will reload immediately.
Is a license tied to a single machine?
License keys are for personal use — one person, reasonable number of machines. There's no technical enforcement of machine limits. If you need a key for a team or enterprise deployment, email to discuss terms.
